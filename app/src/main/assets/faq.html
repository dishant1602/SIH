2. When does the Chardham Yatra Start and Ends ?

Every Year Chardham Yatra begins from the month of April or May onwards and continues until October/November as the doors of all four temples (Badrinath, Kedarnath, Yamunotri & Gangotri) remain open till the day of “Diwali Festival” which is celebrated every year during the month the month of October/November. “Diwali festival –Also called Deepawali '' is considered as one of the main festivals of Hindus and dates of this festival are decided by the holy saints/Gurus/Priests according to Hindu calendar). After Diwali, the doors of all temples remain closed for the next five or six months due to heavy snowfall and very cold weather as all these temples are located in the remote places of Himalayan Mountain where life is not easy to survive due to uncertain weather conditions.

3. What to bring for Chardham Yatra Darshan?

Chardham Yatra is considered as one of the toughest Yatra in Himalayan region, so don’t take it lightly as one should be physically and mentally fit to travel this circuit. Weather of Char Dham Circuit is very uncertain as temperature changes almost three times in a day since day time is hot and evening is pleasant but nights are sometimes very cold. It is advisable to take heavy woolen clothes such as warm jackets, Coat, Sweater, Gloves & woolen socks and good walking shoes with grip, slippers & sandals, first aid kit.

6. What is Char Dham Yatra?

Char Dham Yatra is a holy Hindu pilgrimage. It is a set of four pilgrimage sites in Uttarakhand, India. These are Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. It is believed that these sites help in achieving Moksha (salvation).

8. What is the sequence of Char Dham Yatra?

Char Dham yatra is done in a clockwise direction starting from Yamunotri and ending in Badrinath. However, one should first take a bath in Har Ki Pauri, Haridwar before starting the Yatra. The sequence is Haridwar – Barkot – Yamunotri – Uttarkashi – Gangotri – Ukhimath – Kedarnath – Joshimath – Badrinath – Haridwar.

9. How to register for Char Dham Yatra?

Registration can be done offline at various Biometric Registration Counters located on the route of Char Dham Yatra. People can also register online at home on the official

website for Char Dham. One ID proof (Aadhar, PAN or Passport) and an Email Address are needed for the registration.
